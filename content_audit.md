# Content Audit — Remove Repetition and Simplify Instructions

Repository reviewed: `yukaringorolin/nako-app`

This is a content-only audit. It does not recommend changes to layout, navigation, routes, features, tracking, saved data, Firebase, IDs, or page structure. `src/data.js` was treated as generated output and was not reviewed as a separate source.

## 1. Executive Summary

The audit covered the canonical content in `src/data/`: 167 UI labels, 8 home sections, 64 routines (62 active), 28 recipes, 6 food items, 13 cooking/food-safety items, 24 training commands, 9 training activities, 13 training videos, and 2 additional resources. English, Japanese, and Burmese were compared together, with English used below to identify each item.

Duplication is **moderate and concentrated**, not repository-wide. A normalized scan found 35 exact duplicate groups among 2,007 translated content objects. Most are intentional templates, category labels, accessibility text, or safety reminders. The main actionable repetition is:

- routine photo captions that restate a nearby summary, step, or reminder;
- summaries that contain the whole checklist and are then followed by the same details;
- generic training purpose/instruction text repeated across many commands;
- a few food-safety steps that repeat the card summary almost word for word;
- inactive legacy entries that retain content now covered by active routines.

The most verbose areas are `src/data/routine_tasks.js`, the historical takeaways in `src/data/additional_resources.js`, selected recipe notes in `src/data/recipes.js`, and the `lift-carry` training baseline in `src/data/training.js`. The UI is generally concise; `diaryPrompt` is the clearest exception.

A large rewrite is not necessary. A focused cleanup of roughly 20–35 fields would remove most avoidable reading without changing meaning. Burmese should be rewritten at the same time as English and Japanese, using short spoken-style commands rather than translating long English sentence structures literally. Any medical, emergency, animal-handling, food-safety, appliance-safety, salary/rest-day, or contractual wording should receive human review before application.

## 5. Safety-Critical Repetition

The following repeated wording should remain because the relevant page may be opened independently or because repetition protects Chocho, Nako, Yukari, Edwin, or the household:

- **Window safety:** `general-window-safety`, `nako-supervision`, and `windows-glass-mirrors` repeat no-climbing, no-leaning, grille, and supervision rules. Keep them on each applicable page.
- **Nako emergency and appetite:** the active emergency routine, appetite tracker warning, and contextual resource warning all tell Chocho to contact Edwin. Keep the repeated escalation action.
- **Nako feeding:** retain all quantities (60 g total, about 20 g × 3, exactly 1 topping cube per meal), meal times, water instructions, bowl supervision, and the rule not to change portions without the latest instruction.
- **Kind handling and training:** keep force-free handling on every linked training video and keep “stop if uncomfortable” language. A video may be opened without first reading the global rules.
- **Food storage and leftovers:** keep storage periods, the 2-hour leftovers rule, reheat-once rule, raw poultry discard rule, date labeling, and “when unsure, discard.” Summary-level reinforcement is justified.
- **Raw/cooked separation and raw meat washing:** keep the prohibition, contamination explanation, handwashing, utensil cleaning, lowest-shelf storage, and no-reuse-of-raw-plate rules.
- **Safe thawing and complete cooking:** keep the counter-thawing prohibition, refrigerator/microwave/sealed-cold-water methods, thickest-part check, and uncertainty escalation.
- **Rice, noodles, eggs, and vulnerable people:** keep the overnight warning, toxin warning, cracked-egg rule, and complete-cooking requirement for vulnerable people.
- **Recipes shared around Nako:** keep warnings about onion, garlic, bones, chocolate, chilli, excessive salt, and pet sharing on the individual recipe pages.
- **Appliance and chemical safety:** keep washer unplug/cool/drain/bleach guidance, coffee descaling label guidance, stable-ladder rules, aircon no-dismantling rules, and the fire-extinguisher procedure.
- **Yukari cataplexy:** keep the collapse description, contact instruction, injury/non-recovery escalation, and emergency-help wording complete.
- **Calendar salary/rest day:** keep the 1st-of-month salary date, first-Sunday rest day, and “Calendar entries for exact schedule” wording until a human confirms any revision.

## 6. Priority Recommendations

### High priority

1. Replace full-step photo captions with short visual identifiers in Nako feeding, potty, training, kitchen cleanup, coffee upkeep, weight tracking, linens, mail/deliveries, and the pork-shoulder recipe.
2. Remove the duplicate leading numbers from all 9 washer instructions.
3. Split long multi-action instructions where missing a clause could cause harm: Calendar authority, washer chemicals/filters, Nako emergency handling, and the `lift-carry` safety rule.
4. Shorten routine summaries that currently contain the full checklist, while moving every omitted item into steps.
5. Merge the three fridge-airflow instructions into one direct sentence after human food-safety review.

### Medium priority

1. Shorten recipe notes that repeat times, temperatures, or methods already shown in steps; keep the warning only.
2. Clarify the boundaries between Nako inventory checking and the Shopee cart workflow without changing IDs or cadence.
3. Replace generic training `purpose` text with command-specific purposes in a later dedicated training review.
4. Shorten `lift-carry` history while preserving date, repetition count, support method, and treat status.
5. Verify the four suspicious training-video title/command mappings before editing any video wording.
6. Align `routineCheckInSubtitle` across English, Japanese, and Burmese so all three describe tracked and excluded cadences consistently.

### Low priority

1. Condense the historical dog-and-human takeaways after fact review.
2. Shorten a few 6–8-word routine titles, especially `Upload Photos & Videos to Shared Album`, but keep distinctive recipe and product names.
3. Leave inactive legacy entries unchanged unless maintenance clarity becomes important; they do not currently add reading for Chocho.
4. Keep generic recipe category descriptions and repeated alt text that identifies the same reused image.
